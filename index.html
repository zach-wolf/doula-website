FAQ

A doula is a trained professional who offers emotional, physical, and educational support before, during, and after birth. I’m not a medical provider - I don’t perform clinical tasks - but I am a steady, grounded presence in one of the most sacred seasons of your life.

As your doula, I walk with you through pregnancy, labor, and postpartum - offering encouragement, advocacy, and practical care along the way. Whether you’re preparing for your first birth or your fourth, you don’t have to do it alone. I’ll be in your corner every step of the way.

This is more than a service - it’s a calling. A deep desire to hold space for women in their most powerful and vulnerable moments, and to remind you of what you were made to do.

Studies show that having a doula leads to better outcomes and greater satisfaction with birth. But for me, it goes deeper than that. It’s about having someone beside you who truly sees you, hears you, and stays with you.

I’m here to walk with you through it all - helping you prepare with confidence, offering grounded support during labor, and holding space for your heart and body as you adjust and heal in the postpartum days.

I don’t take the place of your partner - I come alongside them. Your partner knows you. I know birth. Together, we make a steady, trusted team to surround you with care, encouragement, and strength.

Whether you’re birthing at home, in a hospital, or somewhere in between - you don’t have to carry this alone. I’m in it with you.

If you’re looking for full support throughout pregnancy, labor, and the early postpartum weeks, the Birth Doula Package is for you. It’s ideal if you desire hands-on labor support and a deeper relationship leading up to birth.

If you already have birth support (a mother, friend, or partner you feel confident with) but want to prepare well and feel informed going in, the Birth Prep Package is a great fit. It includes focused prenatal meetings, education, and planning - but no in person labor support.

Still unsure? Reach out! I’m happy to chat and help you discern what kind of care best fits your needs.

Yes, I do selectively support hospital births. If you’re planning a hospital birth and feel that we’re a good fit, I’d be honored to support you. My goal is to help you feel informed, empowered, and deeply supported - no matter the setting. We’ll go over your birth preferences together and talk through how to create a peaceful and supported environment, even within the hospital system.

Whether your cesarean is planned or becomes necessary during labor, I will do my best to be present in the OR to support both you and your partner. A cesarean birth is still a birth - and you still deserve advocacy, tenderness, and support.

There are many ways to make your cesarean more gentle and honoring of your desires. We can discuss options like delayed cord clamping, immediate skin-to-skin, dim lighting, a personal playlist, or even clear drapes if your provider allows. These things matter, and you still have a voice in your birth story.

Choosing an epidural doesn’t take away the need - or the value - of a doula. I’ll still support you physically, emotionally, and informationally throughout your labor.

We can still work through different labor positions (yes, even with an epidural!) to encourage progress and keep your body aligned for birth. There’s also so much more to support - your environment, mindset, communication with your care team, informed decision-making, and your partner’s experience too.

My role is never to steer you toward one kind of birth - it’s to walk with you through your birth, offering presence, wisdom, and encouragement every step of the way.

WHY SISTERHOOD?


I chose the name Sisterhood because that’s what I believe birth was always meant to include - a circle of women, a sense of belonging, and support that runs deeper than instruction.

For generations, birth was shared in homes, among mothers, daughters, sisters, and friends. It was sacred. I believe that spirit of sisterhood still matters - and I’ve seen how much it changes a woman’s experience when she knows she’s not alone.

As your doula, I don’t show up as the expert - I show up as your sister. Someone to listen, pray, encourage, and advocate. Someone who will hold space for your story and remind you that you were made for this.

I’ll offer evidence-based education, emotional support, and hands-on guidance - but also gentle reminders of who you are and how capable your body is. You don’t have to do this alone. That’s the beauty of sisterhood - and the heart behind all I do.
